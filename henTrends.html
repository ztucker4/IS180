Layer Hens in California

Though the overall number of layer hens in California has decreased over time, the portion of them that are kept on the largest farms has increased.

The smallest farm category on this chart consists of farms with 1 to 9,999 laying hens. This does consolidate "backyard chickens" (i.e. non-commercial flocks kept in urban/suburban areas by individual residents) and smaller industrial farming operations. Data is available for smaller flocks, but makes up too small a portion to display on this chart. The percent of layer hens on farms with 1-99 hens is currently at its highest-ever value of 0.8%.

Hover over a section of the stacked column chart to see precise numbers of hens for each category and year. The data is taken from a periodic census of farms and represents the total "inventory" of hens at the moment the census was taken.

Data Sources
